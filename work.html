Academic Project


Qualitative Research

My team and I surveyed UTM students to understand how "pocket money" and "peer influence" affect their housing choices, uncovering that financial means and social circles play significant roles in these decisions. This study highlights the importance of considering economic and social factors in developing housing policies and support systems for students. Insights from this research can inform university and housing providers' efforts to align accommodations more closely with student needs, potentially improving student welfare and community cohesion. Despite facing methodological limitations, the findings offer valuable directions for future research and policy-making.

Experimental Research

In our STA305 Experimental Design project, conducted at the University of Toronto Mississauga, my team and I explored the factors influencing the dissolving time of sugar cubes in different liquids at varied temperatures. This study aimed to understand the solubility dynamics of sugar in tap water, soda water, salt water, and lemon juice, across two temperature ranges, 55-65℃ and 85-90℃. Employing a two-way ANOVA, we assessed the impact of liquid type and temperature on dissolution rates, alongside potential interaction effects. Despite limitations like reduced sample size and assumption violations, our findings contribute valuable insights into solubility in practical settings, offering a basis for further research in chemistry and related fields.

Published Game

Eerie Villa

Errie Villa is a narrative-driven deduction game. Players will navigate through different rooms of the villa, interact with various characters, and uncover clues to unravel the story.

I developed "Eerie Villa," a strategy narrative game set in a mysterious mansion, using Twine. It's published on itch.io, blending interactive storytelling with strategic choices that affect the ending. This project highlights my passion for game design and storytelling, inviting players to explore the game's captivating narrative and challenges.
